# Handshake Fit Studio Rubric

Use this rubric to explain how the project evaluates a candidate's fit.

## Resume evidence

The app rewards concrete experience: projects, tools, outcomes, communication, analysis, leadership, technical work, and user-facing support.

## Job-post alignment

The app compares resume language with role and company signals. A strong fit means the resume already contains proof for several important job requirements.

## Keyword coverage

The app identifies matched keywords and missing keywords. Missing terms should only be added when the candidate has real evidence.

## Transferable strength

Not every good fit is a one-to-one title match. The app highlights transferable skills such as communication, analysis, execution, user empathy, technical fluency, and quality judgment.

## Application readiness

A high readiness score means the candidate has enough evidence to tailor a resume, explain the fit, and prepare an interview story. A lower score means the candidate should add proof or choose a better-fit role.

## Cover letter standard

A strong cover letter should connect the candidate's real experience to the company and role. It should sound specific enough that the candidate can defend every sentence in an interview.

## AI trainer proof standard

A strong AI trainer proof sample should show a prompt, two model responses, a preferred response label, and a rationale based on evidence. The evaluator should reward grounded, useful, honest responses and penalize generic or exaggerated claims.
