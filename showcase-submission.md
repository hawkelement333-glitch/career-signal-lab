# SolaRPlexus Project Moonlight Showcase Submission

## Project headline

SolaRPlexus Project Moonlight: a universal resume analyzer that finds your best-fit job and builds a tailored application kit.

## Short description

SolaRPlexus Project Moonlight helps job seekers understand which roles fit their resume best and how to tailor for any company. Users upload or paste their resume, enter a target job and company, and receive role-fit recommendations, a universal company match brief, a hiring-manager brief, an evidence ledger, tailored resume bullets, missing keyword guidance, interview prompts, a company-specific cover letter, a recruiter outreach message, and AI-trainer proof artifacts including a preference rationale, evaluation scorecard, error taxonomy, and rubric matrix.

## What I built

I built a responsive web app with resume upload, resume analysis, universal company targeting, job-family scoring, role match cards, recruiter scan, hiring-manager brief, evidence ledger, signal bars, a fit radar chart, tailored bullet generation, ATS keyword planning, skill-gap recommendations, cover letter drafting, recruiter outreach drafting, cover-letter authenticity audit, AI response-evaluation proof lab, model evaluation scorecard, showcase proof pack, error taxonomy, rubric calibration matrix, interview drill, application guardrails, local autosave, and markdown export.

## Why it matters

Students are often told to tailor their resume, but they are not always shown how. This project turns vague career advice into a concrete workflow: identify fit, prove fit, close gaps, and write application materials that connect real experience to a specific company without inventing claims.

## Responsible AI notes

The prototype runs locally and does not send resume text to a third-party service. It avoids hidden scoring and shows the candidate what matched, what is missing, and what should only be added if they have real evidence. The company matcher is intentionally transparent: it creates targeting prompts for any company name, then asks the user to validate details against the actual job post or company materials.
